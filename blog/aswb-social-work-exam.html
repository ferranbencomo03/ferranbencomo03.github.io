How to pass the ASWB social work exam

For LMSW and LCSW candidates preparing for the ASWB licensing exam.

Most people who struggle with the ASWB exam know the material — they just answer the questions the wrong way. The ASWB rewards a very specific clinical mindset. Learn that, and the content areas, and you put yourself in a strong position to pass.

The "ASWB way" of answering

When several answers look correct, these rules usually point to the best one — in this order:

  1. Safety first. Any risk of harm to self or others is addressed before anything else.
  2. Assess before you act. Gather more information before jumping to an intervention.
  3. Start where the client is and honor self-determination.
  4. Least restrictive option. Choose the least intrusive appropriate action.
  5. Stay in your role — refer out when something is beyond your competence.
  6. When answers seem equal, the NASW Code of Ethics points the way.

What the exam covers

  • Human development, diversity & behavior in the environment (know your theorists).
  • Assessment, diagnosis & treatment planning (biopsychosocial lens, DSM-5-TR overview).
  • Interventions & case management (match the model to the situation — CBT, DBT, MI, crisis, systems).
  • Professional values & ethics — the most testable area of all.

Study tips that work

  • Practice vignette questions and, for each, ask "what's the BEST first step?"
  • Make a one-page sheet of theorists and models — exam items hinge on matching them.
  • Know the NASW core values and key ethical rules (confidentiality, duty to warn, informed consent).
  • Manage your test anxiety — it's a long exam; pace yourself and breathe.
